I have started building OceanTwin AI, a maritime intelligence prototype that connects live vessel tracking, route risk, carbon estimation and knowledge graphs into one decision-support dashboard.

The idea is simple: container shipping does not only need data. It needs connected intelligence.

OceanTwin AI lets a user select a route, monitor watched vessels, estimate ETA risk, understand carbon impact, detect EU maritime exposure and explore the entire scenario as a knowledge graph. I also added a copilot layer that explains why a route is risky and what operational actions could be taken.

This project brings together my interests in maritime technology, data engineering, FAIR data, knowledge graphs and AI-driven decision support.

Tech stack: Python, Streamlit, RDFLib, Plotly, Pandas, WebSockets, AIS-ready data pipeline, semantic knowledge graph modeling.

This is still an MVP, but the direction is clear: smarter maritime operations, better customer visibility and more explainable decision-making in global logistics.
